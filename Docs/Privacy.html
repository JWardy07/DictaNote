Privacy Policy for DictaNote

This policy explains how DictaNote handles data when you record and transcribe notes on iPhone, iPad, and macOS.

Who We Are

DictaNote is a paid productivity app. No account is required to use the app.

Data We Collect

  • Audio recordings you explicitly start in the app.
  • Speech-to-text transcript output.
  • Note metadata such as title, timestamps, and duration.

How We Use Data

  • To record and transcribe your notes.
  • To store notes locally so you can edit, export, and delete them.

Sharing and Third Parties

DictaNote does not include third-party advertising SDKs or analytics SDKs.

"DictaNote does not share your notes, transcripts, or recordings with third-party advertisers, data brokers, or analytics providers."

DictaNote uses Apple's Speech framework for transcription.

"To convert speech to text, DictaNote uses Apple's Speech framework; audio may be transmitted to Apple solely for transcription processing when required by the platform."

Where Data Is Stored

"Your note content is stored locally on your device in the app container and is not uploaded to developer-operated servers."

Notes and recordings are stored in the app's local storage on your device.

Retention and Deletion

"Speech recordings and transcripts are retained until you delete them in the app or uninstall the app."
"When you delete a note, DictaNote deletes that note's locally stored recording file associated with it."

Your Choices

  • You can delete notes at any time in the app.
  • You can revoke microphone and speech permissions in system settings.
  • You can uninstall the app to remove local app data.

Permissions

  • Microphone: required to record voice notes.
  • Speech Recognition: required to convert recorded speech into text.

Policy Updates

If this policy changes, the updated version will be published on this page.
